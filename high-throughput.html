High-Throughput Methods & Robotic Automation

Cutting-edge experimental platforms for rapid discovery and characterization

Revolutionizing Experimental Biology

Our high-throughput methods and robotic automation systems represent the cutting edge of experimental biology. By combining advanced display technologies with automated laboratory workflows, we can screen thousands of molecules, proteins, and interactions in parallel, dramatically accelerating the pace of scientific discovery.

Core Technologies

  • mRNA Display Technology: In vitro display system that links genotype to phenotype, enabling rapid screening of protein libraries with millions of variants.
  • Yeast Display Systems: Eukaryotic display platform for screening protein-protein interactions and antibody-antigen binding with high throughput.
  • Deep Mutational Scanning (DMS): Comprehensive analysis of protein function by systematically testing thousands of mutations in parallel.
  • Automated Laboratory Workflows: Robotic systems that handle sample preparation, screening, and data collection with minimal human intervention.

Method Overview

mRNA Display

In vitro protein evolution and screening

  • Library sizes up to 10^12 variants
  • Rapid selection cycles
  • Compatible with any protein target
  • High-throughput sequencing integration

Yeast Display

Eukaryotic protein display and screening

  • Native protein folding
  • Post-translational modifications
  • Flow cytometry sorting
  • Antibody engineering applications

Deep Mutational Scanning

Comprehensive functional analysis

  • Systematic mutation testing
  • Fitness landscape mapping
  • Evolutionary constraint analysis
  • Disease variant prediction

Robotic Automation

Our laboratory features state-of-the-art robotic systems that automate every aspect of the experimental workflow. From liquid handling and sample preparation to plate reading and data collection, our robots ensure consistent, reproducible results while dramatically increasing throughput.

Data Integration

High-throughput experiments generate massive amounts of data. Our integrated data management system automatically processes, analyzes, and visualizes results, enabling rapid identification of hits and patterns. Machine learning algorithms help identify promising candidates for further characterization.

Applications in Virus Research

These high-throughput methods are particularly powerful for studying virus-immune system interactions. We can screen thousands of antibody variants against viral targets, identify escape mutations, and characterize the functional effects of mutations across entire viral proteins.
